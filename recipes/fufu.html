Fufu

West African fufu is a mixture of boiled cassava root and plantains that are cooked until soft, then mashed until smooth. It's often rounded into balls and served with hot groundnut soup ladled over top.

Ingredients:

  • 4 Yucca roots, peeled and sliced.
  • 5 medium yello plantains, with peels on.
  • 1/2 cup of butter, or more as needed.

Directions:

  1. Gather all ingredients
  2. Place cassava and unpeeled plantains into a large pot. Fill with enough water to cover and bring to a boil.
  3. Cook until cassava is soft, about 30 minutes; drain.
  4. Remove and discard plantain peels from plantains.
  5. Mash cassava and plantains with butter.
  6. Beat with an electric mixer until smooth, adding more butter if needed.
  7. Portion the dough into balls.
